01 — Vision

Vision & Problem Statement

The Vision

NrithyaHolics Online is a platform where any choreographer can teach their original work to learners anywhere in the world — and where any dancer can discover, book, and learn choreography directly from its creator.

The Problem

Great choreographers are limited to teaching in physical studios — constrained by geography, studio availability, and class sizes. Dance learners outside major cities have no access to quality, original choreography instruction. Existing platforms (YouTube, Zoom links, Instagram) are fragmented, lack payment infrastructure, have no quality signals, and offer no community or progression. Platforms like TeamNaach show the demand exists — but they operate as single-brand entities, not open marketplaces.

What Makes This Different

NrithyaHolics is an open marketplace — any choreographer can join, teach, and earn. Sessions are confirmed only when minimum participants register (protecting choreographers from wasted time). The platform handles everything: discovery, booking, payment, live class, recording access, and ratings — so choreographers focus on teaching and learners focus on dancing.

Design Philosophy (v3 Addition)

Functionality over aesthetics. We build what works, not what looks impressive. No custom illustrations, no heavy design assets, no image-heavy layouts that require a designer to maintain. Every UI decision is made with the question: "Does this help a learner book or a choreographer teach?" If not, it's deferred. This keeps the build lean, fast to iterate, and maintainable by a small team.

4.3a Multi-Day / Series Sessions New in v3 — Pulled to MVP

A Series Session is a single bookable unit that spans 2 or more live class dates. The learner books and pays once and gets access to all parts. This is a standard format for dance workshops (e.g. "Learn in 2 sessions over a weekend") and pulled from Phase 2 into MVP scope.

Series Session Rules

Booking: One booking covers the full series. No partial bookings (you cannot buy just Part 2). Admin or choreographer can override this and allow standalone part booking if needed.

Pricing: One price for the full series. The session card shows "2-part series" or "3-part series" badge. Recommended UX: embed all dates in the cover photo (TeamNaach style) + list them in the detail page.

Recording: If a learner misses a part, they still have recording access to that part within the standard free window.

Cancellation: If the minimum seats are not reached 24h before the first part, the entire series is cancelled and all learners are refunded.

Conflict checker: All parts of the series are checked against the platform calendar individually.

4.7 Session Confirmation Logic

Sessions have five states: Draft Open Confirmed Full Cancelled

Open → Confirmed: auto-triggers when min seats is reached. Choreographer notified. Open → Full: when max seats is reached — new visitors see "Join Waitlist" only. Open → Cancelled: if min seats not reached 24 hours before session (or first part of series), auto-cancels. All paid learners receive full credit refund. Choreographer is notified with option to reschedule.

For Series sessions, all logic applies to the series as a whole. Cancellation cancels all parts. Min/max seats are platform-wide for the series.

04a — Identity & Contact Strategy

Identity & Contact Field Strategy New in v3.1

Core Principle

Email is the single source of identity. Always. WhatsApp number and Instagram handle are contact/discovery fields — never login credentials. This prevents duplicate profiles and keeps sign-in frictionless regardless of what contact details are on file.

Why This Matters

WhatsApp number is operationally critical — it's how join links, booking confirmations, and reminders are delivered. Instagram handle is important for choreographer credibility and discovery. But requiring these upfront creates friction that loses sign-ups. The strategy is to capture them progressively at the moments they become naturally valuable — not as a gate to entry.

Preventing Duplicate Profiles — The Email Anchor Rule

This is critical to get right now, before scale makes it painful to fix.

The Rule: One Account = One Email

auth.users is always anchored to email. WhatsApp number is a contact field on the profiles table with a unique constraint — it cannot be shared across two accounts, but it is never used to create a new account or a new auth entry.

When phone/WhatsApp login is added (Phase 2): The flow is: user enters phone → system looks up the email linked to that phone in profiles table → sends magic link to that email. Phone is a lookup key, not an identity anchor. A phone number that isn't linked to any profile gets a "No account found — sign up with email first" message.

If a user tries to link a WhatsApp number already on another account: Show an error — "This number is linked to another account. Contact support if this is an error." Admin can resolve manually.

Mobile as a Feature, Not a Blocker

The guiding principle for WhatsApp number across the platform: having it unlocks things; not having it should never prevent core actions except booking. A user without a WhatsApp number can browse, save sessions, and view choreographer profiles. The one hard gate is completing a booking — because without a number, they cannot receive their join link, making the booking operationally unusable anyway. This is a functional gate, not an arbitrary one, and should be communicated as such.

04b — Virtual Classroom

Virtual Classroom — Full Spec v3.2

Infrastructure: 100ms.live (MVP) → LiveKit (Growth)

MVP: 100ms.live. Free tier covers first ~4–5 sessions/month. React SDK, easy integration, India CDN, adaptive bitrate. Brand-invisible — users never see "100ms".

Phase 2 migration: LiveKit Ship plan at $50/month flat covers ~66 sessions/month — 12× cheaper than 100ms at that scale. Open source, same token architecture, SDK swap is ~2–3 days work. Self-hosted option available long-term for near-zero video COGS.

Session Lifecycle — Full Flow

1
Pre-session screen (–15 min)

Choreo and learners see a "Join Class" button active 15 minutes before start. Screen shows: session cover photo + title, countdown timer, choreo profile card, setup checklist (camera ✅ / mic ✅ / bandwidth result). Learners see: "You'll be muted by default. Camera is required."

2
Lobby — participants wait, cannot see each other

All participants who click "Join" enter a lobby state. They see: session cover photo, countdown timer, "Waiting for your instructor to start the class." No participant video feeds are visible to anyone in the lobby. Participants cannot see or interact with each other. Choreo is not yet in the room.

3
Choreographer joins → admits all

Choreo clicks "Start Class" → enters the room alone first. Sees lobby count: "12 participants waiting." Clicks "Admit All" → all lobby participants are admitted simultaneously. No one-by-one admission. Recording starts automatically at this moment.

4
Live session

Full classroom experience. Choreo controls all interactions. Session timer visible to all (elapsed + remaining based on booked duration).

5
Extension window

At scheduled end time, a banner appears: "Session time is up. Extend by [grace period]?" Choreo clicks Extend → session continues for grace period (admin-configured, default 15 min). Beyond grace period, admin must explicitly approve further extension in AdminPage.

6
Choreo ends session

Clicks "End Class for All." Confirmation dialog. All participants see "Class has ended." Recording stops. Post-session flow triggers automatically.

7
Post-session (automated)

Recording URL captured via webhook → stored on session. Rating prompts sent via WhatsApp to all attendees within 1 hour. Session status → 'completed'. Payout calculation queued (3-day dispute window).

Kick Flow — Complete Spec

Kicking a participant is a serious action with a structured flow, post-kick admin moderation, and discretionary outcomes. The flow is designed to prevent mistakes while keeping it fast enough for a live session.

Step 1 — Choreo initiates kick

Choreo taps a participant tile → "Remove from session" option appears. Must select a reason from a dropdown (admin-configurable list, defaults below).

Default Kick Reasons (admin-configurable)

Step 2 — Confirmation dialog

After selecting reason, a confirmation dialog appears: "You are removing [Learner Name] — Reason: [selected reason]. This person will not be able to rejoin this session. Are you sure?" Two buttons: Cancel / Confirm Remove. This double-check prevents accidental kicks in a live session.

Step 3 — Kick executes

Learner is immediately removed from the room. Their screen shows the reason message (see table above). They cannot rejoin — their booking ID is flagged as kicked for this session. Admin receives a real-time notification in AdminPage: "[Choreo name] removed [Learner name] from [Session title] — Reason: [X] — [timestamp]".

Step 4 — Real-time reinstate (admin only, rare)

If admin determines the kick was a mistake (e.g. choreo accidentally kicked wrong person), admin can reinstate the learner in real-time from AdminPage. Reinstatement generates a new join token for the learner and sends them a WhatsApp message: "You have been reinstated to [Session]. Tap here to rejoin." Choreo is notified: "Admin has reinstated [Learner name]." Admin is supreme — this overrides choreo's decision.

Step 5 — Post-session admin moderation

After the session, admin reviews all kick events in the session log and decides the outcome for each:

Admin DecisionActionRefund?
No further actionKick recorded, no platform-level consequenceAdmin discretion — select reason from refund reason list
Suspend from this choreographer onlyLearner cannot book sessions by this choreographer in futureAdmin discretion
Suspend from platformAccount suspended (SuspendedPage shown on next login)Admin discretion
Wrong kick — choreo errorNo consequence for learner. Note added to choreo's record. If pattern repeats, admin may restrict choreo's kick ability.Full credit refund recommended — admin issues manually

Refund — Admin Discretion Model

Refunds on kick events are never automatic. Admin decides case-by-case and selects a reason from a configurable list when issuing or declining a refund:

Refund reason options (admin-configurable): Instructor error / Technical failure / Wrongful removal / Goodwill gesture / Policy violation by learner — no refund / Ticket sharing confirmed — no refund / Other (free text note).

Refund is issued as credits to learner's account. Admin can also issue partial refund (e.g. 50% for session attended partially before kick).

Session Extension — Revenue Share Impact

Extension Policy

Grace period (admin-configurable, default 15 min, max 30 min): Choreo can extend freely. Revenue share percentage is unchanged during grace period. This is a goodwill buffer — don't penalise choreos for running slightly over.

Beyond grace period: Admin must explicitly approve in AdminPage (approve/deny with one tap). If approved, the extended time is billed at a modified revenue share where NRH's cut increases to cover the additional video infrastructure cost. The specific rate is defined in the revenue share agreement and shown to choreo at session creation.

Rationale: Video cost is per-minute. Payment from learners is per-session. A 30-minute overrun on a 25-person session adds ~₹200 in video cost with no additional revenue. The modified share during admin-approved overrun corrects for this. Choreos are informed of this at session creation — no surprises.

08 — Business Model

Business Model & Payments

Revenue Streams

StreamModelRevenue Split
Live sessionsLearner pays credits. Credits = ₹1 each. Razorpay for credit purchase.Sliding scale. Default NRH 10–20% / Choreo 80–90%

Revenue Share — Sliding Scale

The default sliding scale is set by admin and can be overridden per choreographer. The agreed rate for each session is shown to the choreographer at creation and requires digital sign-off (checkbox + timestamp). Once signed, the rate is locked for that session.

Visibility rule: Choreographer sees only their net amount (tickets sold × their share). Admin sees gross revenue, NRH cut, choreographer cut — full ledger. This is a fixed rule, not configurable.

Payout Flow

After a session is marked completed, a 3-day dispute window opens. If no dispute, choreographer's share is transferred to their registered bank account via Razorpay Payout API. For recording sales (Phase 2), payouts are aggregated and paid monthly on the 5th of each month.

Refund Policy

Full credit refund if session auto-cancels (min seats not met). Learner-initiated cancellation: full credit refund if >48hrs before session; 50% if 24–48hrs; no refund within 24hrs. For series sessions, cancellation applies to the full series. Recording purchases are non-refundable.
